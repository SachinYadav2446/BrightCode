# Voice Input/Output Feature - Quick Summary

## What Was Added

### 🎤 Voice Input (Speech-to-Text)
- Click the **microphone button** (yellow/gold) to start listening
- Speak naturally - your words appear in real-time in the input field
- Click the **microphone button again** to stop listening
- The button turns **red and pulses** while actively listening
- Works continuously - won't stop until you tell it to

### 🔊 Voice Output (Text-to-Speech)
- Bot automatically **speaks its responses** out loud
- Toggle the **speaker icon** in the header to enable/disable
- See a **🔊 indicator** when the bot is speaking
- Voice preference is **saved** across sessions

## How to Use

### Voice Input:
1. Open Pal chatbot
2. Click the microphone button (🎤)
3. Allow microphone access (first time only)
4. Start speaking
5. Watch your words appear in real-time
6. Click mic again to stop, or just click Send
7. Edit if needed, then send

### Voice Output:
1. Click the speaker icon (🔊) in header to toggle
2. When enabled, bot will speak all responses
3. When disabled, bot will only show text
4. Your preference is saved automatically

## Visual Indicators

- **Yellow Mic Button** = Ready to listen
- **Red Pulsing Mic Button** = Actively listening
- **🔊 in Header** = Bot is speaking
- **Gray Mic Button** = Disabled (bot is typing)

## Browser Support

✅ **Works on:**
- Chrome (Desktop & Mobile)
- Edge (Desktop)
- Safari (iOS 14.5+)

❌ **Voice input not supported:**
- Firefox (voice output still works)

## Key Features

✅ Continuous listening (won't stop until you tell it to)  
✅ Real-time transcript (see words as you speak)  
✅ Natural voice synthesis  
✅ Toggle voice output on/off  
✅ Visual feedback with animations  
✅ Automatic text cleaning for better speech  
✅ Preference persistence  
✅ Error handling and fallbacks  

## Tips

- **First time**: Browser will ask for microphone permission - click "Allow"
- **Long messages**: Keep talking, it keeps listening
- **Editing**: You can edit transcribed text before sending
- **Privacy**: Voice processing happens in your browser, not on servers
- **Hands-free**: Great for coding while your hands are on the keyboard
